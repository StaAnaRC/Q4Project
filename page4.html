˗ˏˋ MA. LEA CARMEN SALONGA ˎˊ˗


Ma. Lea Carmen I. Salonga, widely known as Filipino singer Lea Salonga, has been a prominent figure in global performances since she was seven years old. She starred in her first musical, The King and I, in 1978 at the CCP (Cultural Center of the Philippines). Additionally, in 2021, Salonga celebrated her 43rd year in show business. Critics say she is likely one of the greatest performers of all time to hit Broadway.

She has left a legacy as Princess Jasmine singing in the 1992 Disney film Aladdin and Fa Mulan in the 1988 and 2004 Mulan films, marking her place in the Disney Hall of Fame with a Disney Legend Award.

Thus, she has been great at representing Asian heritage abroad, especially in America. She has earned countless awards by delivering the highest-quality performances.

Additionally, Lea Salonga has earned the Olivier, Tony, Drama Desk, Outer Critics Circle, and Theatre World Awards.

However, her performance as Kim, the lead actress in Miss Saigon (1989), became the performance that earned her the renowned Olivier Award for Best Performance by an Actress in a Musical at England’s West End Stage in London.

When the musical moved to Broadway, she was rewarded with her Tony, Drama Desk, Outer Critics Circle, and Theatre World Awards, evolving to be the first Asian woman to win a Tony and the first performer in the world to win that many awards for a single role.

While performing globally, Lea Salonga was also busy creating a music career, especially as her vocals made her unique. She was successful and became the first Filipina music artist to sign to an international record label, marking her place in the industry.
